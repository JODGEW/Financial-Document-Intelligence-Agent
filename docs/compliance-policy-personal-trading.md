# Personal Trading and Securities Disclosure Policy

**Document ID:** CP-2026-003  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Owner:** Compliance Department  
**Last Reviewed:** December 10, 2025  
**Next Review:** December 2026  
**Applies To:** All Covered Persons (see Section 2)

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for personal securities trading and disclosure obligations applicable to all Covered Persons at Acme Corporation. It is designed to prevent conflicts of interest, protect material nonpublic information (MNPI), and ensure compliance with applicable securities laws including Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment, as well as referral to regulatory authorities where required by law.

---

## 2. Covered Persons

This policy applies to:

- All full-time and part-time employees of Acme Corporation
- Members of the Board of Directors
- Consultants and contractors with access to MNPI or client data
- Immediate family members residing in the same household as any of the above

Collectively referred to as **"Covered Persons"** throughout this document.

---

## 3. Restricted Securities

Covered Persons are prohibited from trading in:

1. **Acme Corporation securities** — including common stock, options, warrants, convertible notes, and any derivative instrument referencing Acme securities
2. **Securities on the Restricted List** — a confidential list maintained by the Compliance Department updated no less than weekly; available to Covered Persons upon request through the Compliance portal
3. **Any security about which the Covered Person possesses MNPI** — regardless of whether that security appears on the Restricted List

---

## 4. Pre-Clearance Requirements

### 4.1 When Pre-Clearance Is Required

All Covered Persons must obtain pre-clearance from the Compliance Department **before** executing any trade in:

- Acme Corporation securities
- Any security appearing on the current Restricted List
- Any security about which the Covered Person possesses MNPI

Pre-clearance is **not** required for trades in diversified mutual funds, index ETFs, U.S. Treasury securities, or money market instruments, provided the Covered Person does not possess MNPI relevant to any individual holding within such instruments.

### 4.2 How to Request Pre-Clearance

Pre-clearance requests must be submitted through the **Compliance Portal** at `compliance.acme-internal.com`. Requests must include:

- The security identifier (ticker or CUSIP)
- Proposed transaction type (buy, sell, short sale, option exercise)
- Proposed number of shares or notional value
- Proposed trade date
- Certification that the Covered Person does not possess MNPI relating to the security

### 4.3 Timing

Pre-clearance requests must be submitted **at least 24 hours before the intended trade date**. The Compliance Department will respond within one business day. Silence does not constitute approval.

Approved pre-clearances are **valid for 48 hours** from the time of approval. If the trade is not executed within 48 hours, the Covered Person must submit a new request.

---

## 5. Blackout Periods

### 5.1 Quarterly Blackout

A trading blackout for Acme Corporation securities applies to all Covered Persons during the following windows each quarter:

- Beginning **two weeks before** the last day of each fiscal quarter, and
- Ending **48 hours after** the public release of Acme's quarterly or annual earnings

During a blackout period, no Covered Person may buy, sell, or otherwise transact in Acme Corporation securities, regardless of whether pre-clearance was previously obtained.

### 5.2 Event-Driven Blackouts

The Compliance Department may impose additional event-driven blackout periods in connection with material corporate transactions, regulatory developments, or other circumstances involving MNPI. Covered Persons will be notified of event-driven blackouts by direct communication from the Compliance Department. The existence or duration of an event-driven blackout is itself confidential.

---

## 6. Disclosure and Reporting

### 6.1 Initial Holdings Report

Each Covered Person must submit an **Initial Holdings Report** within 10 calendar days of becoming subject to this policy, disclosing all personal securities accounts and holdings in covered securities.

### 6.2 Annual Holdings Report

All Covered Persons must submit an **Annual Holdings Report** by January 31 of each year, disclosing all personal securities accounts and holdings as of December 31 of the prior year.

### 6.3 Transaction Reports

All Covered Persons must report each personal securities transaction subject to this policy within **5 business days** of execution. Transaction reports must include the date, security, transaction type, quantity, and price.

All reports are submitted through the Compliance Portal.

---

## 7. Prohibited Practices

Regardless of pre-clearance status, the following practices are prohibited at all times:

- **Short-term trading:** Buying and selling (or selling and buying) the same security within 60 calendar days
- **Short selling** of Acme Corporation securities
- **Hedging transactions** involving Acme Corporation securities, including through put options, collars, or prepaid variable forwards
- **Pledging** Acme Corporation securities as collateral for a loan
- **Tipping:** Disclosing MNPI to any person who may trade on that information

---

## 8. Enforcement and Consequences

The Compliance Department has authority to:

- Review all pre-clearance requests and deny approval without explanation
- Require disgorgement of profits from trades that violate this policy
- Require cancellation or reversal of a trade that violates this policy
- Impose trading suspensions on any Covered Person pending investigation

Violations of this policy will be reported to the General Counsel and, where required by applicable law, to the relevant regulatory authority.

---

## 9. Questions and Exceptions

Questions regarding this policy should be directed to the Compliance Department at `compliance@acme-corp.com` or by submitting an inquiry through the Compliance Portal.

Requests for exception to any requirement of this policy must be submitted in writing to the Chief Compliance Officer. Exceptions will be considered only in extraordinary circumstances and will not be granted retroactively.
